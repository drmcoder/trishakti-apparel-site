Aim at buyers who need **500–2,000 pcs, custom design, duty-free** — and who will trial-order
BEFORE demanding a full audit (since our SMETA audit isn't done yet). Three segments below,
each with WHERE to find them, HOW to approach, and search terms you can paste today.

They already trust the Nepal model → easiest conversation ("extra / backup capacity, faster lead").

## SEGMENT B — Growing startup brands (scaling, need reliable production)
The sweet spot: past launch, growing, outgrowing tiny local makers, need 500–2,000 pcs runs.
**Where to find:**
- **Crowdfunding** — Kickstarter / Indiegogo → Fashion category → *funded* apparel campaigns.
  A funded campaign = a brand that MUST produce now. Message the founder.
- **Instagram** — search hashtags: `#ethicalfashion` `#organiccotton` `#slowfashion`
  `#newclothingbrand` `#sustainablefashion`. Target brands with **5k–50k followers**
  (big enough to order, small enough to reply). DM the founder + find their email.
- **Shopify / Etsy** sellers scaling into custom manufacturing.
- **LinkedIn** — search `founder` + `apparel brand` / `private label`; connect + short note.
**How to approach:** Instagram DM or email (warm, short), then line-card PDF.
**Template:** A/B, but lead with "we grow WITH small brands — flexible MOQ, we won't put you behind a mega-buyer."

## SEGMENT C — Resellers & wholesale buyers
Buy to resell → repeat volume, less design-heavy, faster to close.
- **Promotional-products / merch companies** — they resell custom tees/hoodies to corporates.
  Search `custom apparel supplier promotional products`, or ASI / PPAI member companies.
- **Blank & wholesale apparel distributors** — want a private-label/custom source.
- **Print-on-demand shops** scaling from POD to bulk custom manufacturing.
- **Multi-brand boutiques / retailers** wanting their own private label.
**Directories to mine (real buyer lists):**
- Europages — "T-shirt importer": europages.co.uk/companies/t%20shirt%20importer.html
- Fibre2Fashion buyers: fibre2fashion.com/garments
- Foursource (create a supplier profile → buyers send RFQs)
- Kompass, Kellysearch (importer directories)
**Template:** A/B, lead with **price + duty-free landed cost + MOQ + repeat capacity**.

## THE FILTER (don't waste emails)
For each lead, 30-second check before you contact:
1. Do they SELL finished clothing (buyer) — or MAKE it (competitor, skip)?
2. Do they own their own factory already? (skip)
3. Is there a real contact (email / IG DM / form)?
4. Would 500–2,000 pcs fit their size? (too big = they use mega-factories; too tiny = can't meet MOQ)
→ If yes to 1–4, add to the tracker and send Template A/B + line-card PDF.

## Realistic numbers
B2B cold outreach converts at ~1–5%. To land 1–2 real buyers, plan to contact **30–50**
qualified leads. Three sent so far → keep filling the pipeline. Consistency wins this.
