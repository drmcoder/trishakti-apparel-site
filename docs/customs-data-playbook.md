# Customs import-data targeting playbook (2026-07-20)

Find buyers who ALREADY import knitwear, with their real volumes, then pitch them the
duty-free Nepal angle using their own sourcing facts. Near-zero guessing.

⚠️ **Data limits:** US sea shipments only (no air, no road, **no EU/UK importer data** in the free set). Some records show a **freight forwarder** instead of the real importer — cross-check names.

## THE 3 CORE TECHNIQUES

### 1. ⭐ Reverse-competitor lookup (FREE, highest ROI)
The free tier searches **by company name** — so use it on your COMPETITORS:
1. Take a big **India/Bangladesh knit exporter's** name (list below)
2. Search it in ImportYeti → you see the **US companies importing from them** = their customers
3. Those buyers already buy **exactly your product at scale** → pitch: *"same knit, duty-free from Nepal"*

**Seed list of big knit exporters to reverse-look-up (their customers = your targets):**
- **India (Tiruppur knit hub):** Shahi Exports, KPR Mill, Best Corporation, SCM Garments, Eastman Exports, Pratibha Syntex, Gokaldas Exports
- **Bangladesh:** Ha-Meem Group, DBL Group, Fakir Fashion, Beximco, Pacific Jeans (knit arms)
> Each search returns a ready-made list of proven knit importers.

### 2. HS-code product search (Pro, or paid tools)
Search knit HS codes to list ALL importers of that product:
- **6109** (t-shirts), **6110** (sweaters/hoodies), **6105/6106** (knit shirts/blouses), **6114** (other knit)
- Filter by origin = India/Bangladesh/China → importers you can win with duty-free Nepal

### 3. Brand validation (arm your pitch)
Before emailing a target, search **their name** → confirm what/how much they import and from where. Then your pitch cites real facts.

## Turn data into outreach (tactfully)
Reference their sourcing **lightly** — don't creep them out by quoting private-looking data:
> ✅ "I understand you source knitwear from South Asia — Nepal offers the same, duty-free into the EU/UK…"
> ❌ "Your records show 47 containers from Shahi Exports last year…" (too much — feels surveillance-y)

Then: find the contact (website / LinkedIn sourcing manager), send Template A/B + line-card.

## First session — do this
1. Open **importyeti.com** (free account)
2. Search 3–4 exporters from the seed list → export the importer lists
3. Pick the **top 15 by volume**, find each contact
4. Draft personalised pitches (I can write these once you have the names)

## Caveats / honesty
- Free = US sea only; **EU/UK buyers won't appear** → for those, use LinkedIn + the brand research we already did.
- Air shipments and small parcels aren't in the data (misses some DTC brands).
- Forwarder names can mask the real importer — verify before pitching.
- US is not duty-free for Nepal apparel → for US targets, lead with **quality + tariff-relative-to-India + ethics**, not duty-free. (Duty-free pitch is for their EU/UK/CA/AU arms if they have them.)
