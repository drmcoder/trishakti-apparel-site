# Market Feasibility — Exporting Trishakti Apparel knit basics

_Analysis date: 2026-07-13. Product scope: knit t-shirts & polos (HS 6109/6105/6106/6110),
fabric imported from China, cut & sewn in Nepal, ~100 machines, EXIM held / REX in progress._

> **Verdict: FEASIBLE, but conditionally — as a niche small-batch private-label supplier,
> NOT as a low-cost bulk commodity mill.** The duty-free story you're building the site
> around is real but weaker than it looks, because your closest competitors get the same
> zero duty *and* pay lower wages. Your win comes from flexible MOQ + own-design/CAD +
> quality + a closing time window — not from being cheapest. Read the "uncomfortable
> truths" section before committing marketing spend.

---

## 2. Where you actually WIN vs. where you LOSE

**You win on:**
- **Flexible / low MOQ (300–500 pcs).** Bangladesh mega-factories want 5,000–10,000+ pcs
  per style. Small private-label brands, startups, and boutique labels are stranded by that
  — this is your real market gap.
- **Own designs + in-house Optitex CAD, marking, resizing.** You're not just a contract
  sewer; you can take a sketch to spec. Most small Nepali sewing units cannot.
- **Quality + agility** on short, changeable runs.
- **US relative-tariff window (right now).** Nepal's +10% is the *lowest* add-on in South
  Asia (India far higher). For a US buyer switching away from India/China, Nepal is
  suddenly attractive — this is a genuine, time-limited opening.

**You lose on:**
- **Price at volume.** Bangladesh pays lower wages *and* gets the same EU zero-duty. On a
  plain 10,000-pc tee order you cannot beat Dhaka. Don't try.
- **Logistics.** Nepal is landlocked — bulk routes via **Kolkata port**, adding freight
  cost and ~1–2 weeks vs. a Chittagong or Ho Chi Minh factory. Real, permanent handicap.
- **Ecosystem depth.** Nepal's whole garment export sector is small (~$86M in 2022 vs a
  $222M peak in 2003). Thin trims/fabric supply locally → you import from China → longer
  material lead times than integrated hubs.
- **No social-compliance audit yet** (BSCI/Sedex/SMETA). This is the single biggest
  deal-killer for serious buyers, who require it before placing orders.

---

## 3. Uncomfortable truths to correct in the current strategy

1. **The "~12% duty-free edge" is not a Nepal-only edge.** Bangladesh, Cambodia, and
   Myanmar also enter the EU at 0% (EBA); Vietnam, Turkey, Pakistan (GSP+) at 0% via
   FTA/GSP+. So duty-free is **table stakes among low-cost suppliers, not a moat.** Your
   12% saving is only vs. a buyer currently sourcing from a *non-preferential* origin
   (e.g. plain-MFN China). Keep the duty-free hook — but frame it as "0% *and* small MOQ
   *and* own design," never as a standalone price weapon.
2. **Nepal is not the cheapest labor.** You are ~25% *more* expensive per worker than
   Bangladesh. Positioning as "low cost" invites a comparison you lose. Position as
   "right-sized, high-touch, duty-free."
3. **The EU duty-free window is closing on a clock.** Graduation 24 Nov 2026 → EBA
   transition to ~2029 → then either **GSP+ (0%, but Nepal must ratify 27 conventions)**
   or **standard GSP (0–4.5% on knits)** or **MFN (12%)**. Buyers signing multi-year
   programs will ask "what happens in 2029?" — have an answer (GSP+ track).

## 4. Market-by-market feasibility

| Market | Duty on your tee | Feasibility | Play |
|---|---|---|---|
| **EU (27)** | 0% now (EBA) → transition to 2029 → GSP+ target | **High** | Hero market. Small private-label brands. Use the 2029 clock as urgency. |
| **UK** | 0% under UK DCTS (LDC scheme), independent of EU | **High** | Same pitch; UK kept an LDC-style preference post-Brexit. Verify current DCTS terms. |
| **USA** | MFN ~13.6% + 10% reciprocal, BUT lowest add-on in region | **Medium-High, time-sensitive** | Ride the "cheaper than India/China tariff" moment. Buyers diversifying out of Asia. |
| **Australia / Canada / Japan** | LDC preferences generally apply | **Medium** | Secondary; pursue opportunistically once EU/US traction proven. |
| **India (across the border)** | Nepal–India treaty access | **Medium** | Nearest, cheapest logistics; lower margin but easy volume filler. |

---

## 5. Timing — why "now" is genuine, not marketing

- **EU:** duty-free guaranteed only through ~2029. A buyer who onboards Nepal in 2026 gets
  ~3 years of certainty + a GSP+ continuation path. "Lock it in now" is truthful.
- **US:** the relative-tariff advantage vs. India exists *today* and is policy-dependent —
  it can change. Buyers hunting alternatives to high-tariff origins are actively looking
  right now.
- Both windows argue for **fast outreach in the next 6–12 months**, not a slow build.

---

## 6. Go / No-Go recommendation

**GO — but as a focused niche play, with two must-fix gates.**

**Target buyer profile (aim the whole site + outreach here):**
- Small-to-mid **private-label brands**, DTC/e-commerce labels, boutique fashion, promo/
  merch companies, and **sourcing agents serving them** — buyers who need 300–2,000 pcs
  per style, custom design, and duty-free landed cost. NOT commodity bulk importers.
- Geography priority: **EU → UK → US** (in that order for margin & duty logic).

**Two gates that unlock most serious buyers (do these before scaling outreach):**
1. **Get REX number + Certificate of Origin setup live** (REX already in progress via TEPC
   — finish it). Without valid origin proof, the duty-free pitch is unusable.
2. **Start a social-compliance audit path** (BSCI/Sedex/SMETA). Even "audit booked for
   Q4" converts far better than "none." This is the #1 conversion blocker, per your own
   research.

---

## 7. Key risks (rank-ordered)

1. **Compliance gap** — no audit → serious buyers won't place orders. _Mitigate: book audit._
2. **2029 EU cliff** — duty-free not permanent. _Mitigate: pursue GSP+ eligibility; be honest with buyers._
3. **Cost competition from Bangladesh** — if you chase bulk. _Mitigate: refuse the bulk race; stay niche._
4. **Landlocked freight cost/time** — via Kolkata. _Mitigate: quote FOB Kolkata clearly; bake it into pricing._
5. **Thin local supply chain** — China fabric lead time. _Mitigate: hold safety stock of core fabrics; set honest lead times._
6. **Single-country / single-product concentration** — knit basics only. _Mitigate: prove one lane before widening._
