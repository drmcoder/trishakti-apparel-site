Why this matters: duty is a 12–18% margin lever

For a knit t-shirt, import duty is one of the biggest hidden costs in your landed price. Source from a non-preferential country and you pay 12% (EU/UK) or 18% (Canada) on top of the garment. Source the same garment from a duty-free origin and that cost is zero — straight back into your margin or your shelf price. On a $500,000/year program into the EU that's ~$60,000 a year; into Canada, ~$90,000.

This guide shows which markets give Nepal 0%, why imported fabric still qualifies, how long the window lasts, and — honestly — where Nepal does and doesn't beat the alternatives.

2The key that unlocks it: “single transformation”

Most buyers assume “if my fabric is imported from China, I lose duty-free.” For Nepal, that's wrong in the markets that matter. Under LDC rules for the EU, UK, Canada and Japan, the garment being cut and sewn in Nepal confers Nepali origin — regardless of where the fabric came from. Canada went furthest: since January 2025, cut & sew in an LDC confers origin no matter the fabric or yarn origin.

Translation: Chinese fabric + made in Nepal = “Made in Nepal” = 0% duty into the EU, UK, Canada and Japan. You supply nothing extra except a Certificate of Origin (we provide it).

Note: for the US and standard GSP schemes, stricter “double transformation” can apply — imported fabric may not qualify. That's why the market matters.

3The clock: a real window, closing ~2029

Nepal is scheduled to graduate from Least Developed Country status on 24 November 2026 (a further 3-year deferral has been requested and is under review). Either way: the EU & UK grant a 3-year transition from graduation, so duty-free access holds to ~2029. After that, access moves toward standard GSP with stricter rules of origin — under which imported fabric may no longer qualify. The UK (DCTS) preserves single transformation even into its Enhanced Preferences tier, making it the most durable duty-free route.

Bottom line: there's a genuine 2–3 year window to lock in duty-free Nepal sourcing. Onboard now and you get certainty plus a transition runway.

5How to actually claim the duty-free rate

1. Certificate of Origin — proves “Made in Nepal.” Your Nepali manufacturer arranges it (we do).
2. Statement on Origin / REX — for the EU, small consignments (< €6,000) can use a self-declared Statement on Origin; larger orders use a REX-registered exporter number.
3. Correct HS code + product spec on your commercial invoice, matched to the destination's rule.

Your manufacturer handles the origin paperwork — you just confirm your destination and product.

6Quick checklist: is duty-free Nepal right for you?

  • You sell into the EU, UK, Canada or Japan (where Nepal is 0%)
  • You currently source from China or India (paying 12–18% you could save)
  • You want runs of 500–5,000/style (not 20,000+)
  • You value ethical, transparent production
  • You want to de-risk away from a single sourcing country

3+ ticks? Duty-free Nepal is worth a sample.
