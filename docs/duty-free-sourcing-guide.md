## Why this matters: duty is a 12–18% margin lever
For a knit t-shirt, import duty is one of the biggest hidden costs in your landed price.
Source from a non-preferential country and you pay **12% (EU/UK), 18% (Canada)** on top of the
garment. Source the *same* garment from a duty-free origin and that cost is **zero** — straight
back into your margin or your shelf price. On a $500,000/year program into the EU, that's **~$60,000
a year.** Into Canada, **~$90,000.**

This guide shows you exactly which markets give Nepal 0%, why imported fabric still qualifies,
how long the window lasts, and — honestly — where Nepal does and doesn't beat the alternatives.

## 2. The key that unlocks it: "single transformation"
Most buyers assume *"if my fabric is imported from China, I lose duty-free."* **For Nepal, that's wrong
in the markets that matter.** Under LDC rules for the EU, UK, Canada and Japan, the garment being
**cut and sewn in Nepal** confers Nepali origin — **regardless of where the fabric came from**
("single transformation"). Canada went furthest: since **January 2025**, cut & sew in an LDC confers
origin no matter the fabric or yarn origin.

> **Translation:** Chinese fabric + made in Nepal = "Made in Nepal" = 0% duty into the EU, UK, Canada
> and Japan. You supply nothing extra except a **Certificate of Origin** (we provide it).

*(Note: for the US and for standard GSP schemes, stricter "double transformation" can apply — imported
fabric may not qualify. That's why the market matters.)*

## 3. The clock: a real window, closing ~2029
Nepal is scheduled to graduate from Least Developed Country status on **24 November 2026** (a further
3-year deferral has been requested and is under review). Either way:
- **EU & UK** grant a **3-year transition** from graduation → duty-free access holds to **~2029**.
- After that, access moves toward standard GSP/GSP+ with **stricter rules of origin** — under which
  imported fabric may no longer qualify.
- **The UK (DCTS) preserves the single-transformation rule even into its Enhanced Preferences tier** —
  making it the most durable duty-free route.

**Bottom line:** there's a genuine 2–3 year window to lock in duty-free Nepal sourcing. Buyers who
onboard now get certainty *and* a transition runway.

## 5. How to actually claim the duty-free rate (3 steps)
1. **Certificate of Origin** — proves "Made in Nepal." Your Nepali manufacturer arranges it (we do).
2. **Statement on Origin / REX** — for the EU, small consignments (< €6,000) can use a self-declared
   Statement on Origin; larger orders use a REX-registered exporter number.
3. **Correct HS code + product spec** on your commercial invoice, matched to the destination's rule.

*Your manufacturer handles the origin paperwork — you just confirm your destination and product.*
